Every week there's a new announcement about AI tools for nonprofits. Automated grant writing. AI intake forms. Intelligent scheduling.

For a well-staffed shelter with existing systems, some of these are worth evaluating. For a small rescue with two coordinators and forty animals, they're a distraction from more urgent problems.

## What small rescues actually need

Before AI, small rescue teams need these things to work:

**Reliable animal records.** Who is this animal? When did they come in? What's their status? Where are they now? These questions should be answerable in five seconds, not fifteen minutes of spreadsheet archaeology.

**Clear task tracking.** Which animals need what, by when? Who is responsible? What's overdue? A simple, honest task list beats a sophisticated AI suggestion system.

**Medical record continuity.** When an animal moves from the rescue's care to an adopter's vet, the vaccination history should go with them. This requires clean data entry, not AI.

**Lost/found record tracking.** If someone calls about a found dog, you need to be able to search quickly and reliably. Fast search on clean data beats AI matching on messy data.

**Grant deadline visibility.** Knowing that three grant deadlines land in October is a basic operational need. A calendar view of deadlines helps more than AI narrative drafting.

## The right order

Build the foundation first:
1. Clean data entry
2. Reliable status tracking
3. Role-appropriate access control
4. Search that works
5. Data export

Then consider AI assistance:
1. Adoption bio drafting
2. Grant narrative drafting
3. Intake note summarization
4. Lost/found description matching

AI is most useful when it assists humans who understand the context. It's least useful when it's substituting for the operational foundation that should exist first.

## What Open Paw prioritizes

Open Paw's V1 is focused on the foundation. Getting animal records right. Making the status pipeline clear. Keeping the foster workflow organized. Building the grant tracking structure.

The AI assistance features are being built as a layer on top of a functional operations system — not as a substitute for one.

This is the right order.
