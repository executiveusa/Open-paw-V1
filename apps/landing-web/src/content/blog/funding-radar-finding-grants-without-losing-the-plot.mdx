If you've tried to research animal welfare grants, you know the feeling. A spreadsheet with 40 funders, a folder of PDFs, and a nagging sense that you missed a deadline somewhere.

Funding Radar is Open Paw's attempt to fix that.

## The problem with grant research

Grant research is painful for animal welfare organizations for a few reasons:

**Funders are scattered.** Petco Love, ASPCA, PetSmart Charities, your state's community foundation, local pet supply company foundations — there's no single place to find them all.

**Eligibility is murky.** Many foundations post vague eligibility requirements. Figuring out whether you qualify for a grant often requires reading between the lines of dense application guidelines.

**Deadlines creep up.** Grant deadlines are not evenly distributed through the year. When five deadlines land in October, something gets missed.

**Applications take real time.** A grant narrative isn't something you can bang out in an afternoon. It requires impact data, program descriptions, budget justifications, and careful editing.

## What Funding Radar does

Funding Radar approaches this as a workflow problem, not just a database problem.

The workflow looks like this:

1. **Build your organization profile.** Species you serve, geographic focus, programs you run, tax status, annual budget.
2. **Match to opportunities.** Funding Radar surfaces grants that match your profile.
3. **Check eligibility.** Before you spend time drafting, verify that you meet the requirements.
4. **Create an application workspace.** One place for the narrative draft, budget, checklist, and submission status.
5. **Track reporting obligations.** Awarded grants have reporting deadlines too.

## The AI guardrails

AI can help draft grant narratives. But AI assistance in grant writing requires strict guardrails.

Funding Radar's AI features will never:
- Submit an application automatically
- Fabricate program statistics
- Invent partnerships or endorsements
- Modify budget numbers without approval
- Claim nonprofit status unless your profile confirms it

Every AI output is clearly marked as a draft. Humans review and approve before anything goes out.

## The current state

In V1, Funding Radar includes the data model, funder database, grant opportunity tracking, and application workspace. The AI drafting assistant and automated grant ingestion are on the roadmap for the next milestone.

The foundation is in place. The goal is to make grant research feel less like archaeology and more like a manageable workflow.
