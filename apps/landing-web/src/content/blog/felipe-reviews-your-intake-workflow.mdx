title: Felipe Reviews Your Intake Workflow
description: Felipe — Open Paw's unofficial mascot — has opinions about your intake form. Strong ones.
date: 2024-03-18
category: Product
---

Felipe is a 4-year-old orange tabby who came into the Open Paw project as an unofficial mascot and occasional critic of poorly designed intake workflows.

He has some notes.

---

**On your intake form being 47 fields long:**

The first fields should be: name (even a temp name), species, and where they came from. Everything else can wait. If a field cannot be filled in during a 10-minute intake appointment, it should be optional and clearly labeled as such. You don't need the microchip number, the medical history, and the surrender reason before the cat is even in the building.

**On having three different spreadsheets for intake:**

Felipe understands that the first spreadsheet made sense. The second one also made sense, at the time. The third one was probably created at 11pm by someone who was desperate. The solution is not a fourth spreadsheet.

**On the "private notes" field being optional when it should be required:**

Some animals come in with context that matters: behavioral history, abuse flags, legal holds. The intake form should make it easy to add this information, and the display logic should make sure it's never accidentally shown to the wrong people. A single "notes" field that shows up everywhere is not a private notes system.

**On the status field having 22 possible values:**

Felipe counted. Fourteen of them appear to be variants of "kind of available but not really." The status pipeline should be clear enough that any volunteer can understand what a status means without asking. If you need a legend, simplify the statuses.

**On the intake form that hasn't been updated since 2018:**

The rescue has changed. The software probably hasn't. This is normal, but it's worth fixing. An intake form that was designed for cats-only but now handles dogs, rabbits, and the occasional goat is not serving anyone well.

---

Felipe's overall assessment: intake is the foundation. If the data going in is messy, everything downstream is harder — finding fosters, tracking status, generating adoption bios, writing impact reports for grants.

It's worth getting right.

He's available for further consultation in exchange for appropriate treats.
