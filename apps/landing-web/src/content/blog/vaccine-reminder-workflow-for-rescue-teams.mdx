Vaccination tracking is one of those things that seems simple until you're managing 50 animals with different intake dates, different vaccination histories, and different timelines to completion.

Missing a booster isn't just a medical risk — it delays adoption, complicates transport, and can create liability issues. But most rescue teams don't have a practice management system. They have a spreadsheet.

## What rescue vaccine tracking actually needs

Rescue teams don't need the full complexity of veterinary practice management. They need:

- A record of what was given, when, and by whom
- An alert when a follow-up is due
- A way to see, at a glance, which animals are behind on vaccines
- Export-ready records for the adopting family and the receiving vet

## The Open Paw approach

In Open Paw, vaccination records are stored in the medical record system, tied to the animal. Each vaccination record includes:

- Vaccine type
- Date administered
- Administered by (vet name or clinic)
- Lot number (optional)
- Next due date (calculated or manual)

Tasks can be linked to medical records. When a booster is due in 21 days, a task is created. The task shows up on the coordinator's board. The coordinator can delegate it to the appropriate person.

## The simple workflow

**At intake:** Record initial vaccines given. Create follow-up tasks for boosters.

**At each medical visit:** Add the vaccination record. Mark the follow-up task complete if applicable. Create the next reminder task.

**Before adoption:** Run the vaccination status check. Confirm all required vaccines are current. Generate the vaccination history for the adopter.

## What this doesn't replace

This is not a full veterinary EMR. Diagnosis, prescriptions, lab results, and treatment planning require real practice management software and a licensed veterinarian.

Open Paw's medical module is designed for rescue and shelter teams who need reliable tracking without the full complexity (and cost) of veterinary software.
